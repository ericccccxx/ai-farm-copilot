# Investor Brief

## Thesis

AI agents are becoming a new software distribution channel. Agriculture, turf, and land management are expensive, fragmented, and heavily dependent on expert workflows. AI Farm Copilot turns those workflows into an open-source, agent-native system that can start with simple phone photos and grow into cameras, drones, sensors, robots, and equipment integrations.

## Product

AI Farm Copilot is an open-source AI farm and garden assistant for vineyards, orchards, golf turf, hobby farms, and home gardens. It packages field inspection workflows into an AI skill that can be used by Codex, Claude, and other agents.

## Why Now

- AI agent workflows are becoming mainstream.
- GitHub is becoming a distribution layer for reusable AI capabilities.
- Precision agriculture and turf monitoring are expensive and fragmented.
- Low-cost cameras, drones, and sensors are easier to deploy than ever.
- Hardware companies need decision intelligence, not just device control.

## Wedge

Start with a free open-source agent skill and local demo. Use GitHub stars, installs, pilot users, and field reports as adoption signals. Then expand into hosted dashboards, camera/sensor kits, deployment services, and hardware partnerships.

## Initial Markets

- Vineyards.
- Orchards.
- Golf courses.
- Hobby farms.
- Home gardens.
- Agriculture and robotics hardware companies.

## Business Model

- Free open-source core.
- Hosted dashboard.
- Reporting packages.
- Camera and sensor kits.
- Deployment and integration services.
- Enterprise and hardware partnerships.

## What We Need To Prove

- Users will install and try the open-source workflow.
- Users will upload photos and field notes.
- The reports are useful enough to repeat weekly.
- Commercial operators want saved reports and hardware integration.
- Hardware partners see it as a useful AI decision layer.
